# RFBA Literature Review

This document tracks published scientific research, technical reports, historical reactor concepts, patents, and other prior work relevant to the Regenerative Fusion Boundary Architecture (RFBA) research hypothesis.

## Research Areas

The literature review will investigate:

- Plasma-facing materials
- Liquid-metal first walls
- Liquid lithium systems
- Molten-salt blankets
- FLiBe and other candidate blanket materials
- Silicon carbide (SiC/SiC) composites
- Radiation and neutron-induced material damage
- Self-healing and self-renewing materials
- Sacrificial and replaceable plasma-facing surfaces
- Magnetohydrodynamic (MHD) effects
- Tritium breeding and extraction
- Heat transfer and coolant systems
- Fusion reactor digital twins
- AI-assisted plasma and reactor control
- Predictive maintenance
- Autonomous reactor operation
- Biomimetic engineering
- Artificial homeostasis
- Integrated multiphysics reactor optimization

## Literature Classification

Each source reviewed will be classified as one or more of the following:

- **Established** — Supported by substantial theoretical and experimental evidence.
- **Experimentally Demonstrated** — Demonstrated experimentally but not necessarily commercially deployed.
- **Active Research** — Currently under significant investigation.
- **Theoretically Proposed** — Proposed or simulated but lacking sufficient experimental validation.
- **Historical Concept** — Previously investigated concept that may no longer be actively pursued.
- **Potential Research Gap** — An area that appears insufficiently explored based on the sources reviewed.
- **Unknown / Unverifiable** — Information that cannot currently be verified through accessible sources.

## Research Integrity

The absence of publicly accessible prior work will not be treated as proof that an idea is novel.

The review will attempt to consider peer-reviewed literature, government and laboratory publications, conference proceedings, patents, dissertations, historical documents, accessible non-English research, and references to non-digitized sources where possible.

Classified research, proprietary corporate research, unpublished work, and inaccessible physical literature may exist beyond the scope of this review.
